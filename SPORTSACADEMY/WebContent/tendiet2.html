Tennis Player Diet Plan

Meal Food Options (India)
Breakfast
  • Option 1: Oatmeal with fresh fruits (e.g., banana or apple) and nuts, a glass of milk or yogurt
  • Option 2: Whole-grain toast with peanut butter, boiled eggs, and a fruit smoothie
Mid-Morning Snack Handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts), Greek yogurt with honey
Lunch
  • Option 1: Grilled chicken or tofu with brown rice, saut√©ed vegetables, and a mixed greens salad
  • Option 2: Roti (whole wheat flatbread) with dal (lentils), mixed vegetable curry, and curd
Afternoon Snack Whole-grain crackers with hummus, carrot and cucumber sticks
Pre-Training Snack Banana or apple, whole-grain sandwich with chicken or paneer (cottage cheese) and veggies
During Training/Match Stay hydrated with water or a sports drink with electrolytes
Post-Training/Match
  • Option 1: Protein shake (whey or plant-based), quinoa or sweet potato, grilled fish or tofu, and steamed broccoli
  • Option 2: Lassi (yogurt-based drink) with a protein-rich snack like sprouts or roasted chana (chickpeas)
Dinner
  • Option 1: Baked salmon or a plant-based protein, quinoa or brown rice, steamed asparagus, and a mixed greens salad
  • Option 2: Khichdi (rice and lentil porridge) with a side of curd and boiled vegetables
Evening Snack Curd (yogurt) with fresh seasonal fruits or a small piece of cheese, herbal tea or warm milk (to aid sleep)

General Tips:

  • Focus on locally available and easily understandable foods.
  • Prioritize whole grains (roti, rice), lean proteins (chicken, tofu), vegetables, and fruits.
  • Minimize sugary and processed foods.
  • Maintain balanced meals.
  • Pay attention to portion sizes.
  • Stay well-hydrated with water or homemade beverages.
  • Allow time for digestion before intense training or matches.
