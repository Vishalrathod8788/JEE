Cricket Player Diet Plan

Meal Food Options (India)
Breakfast
  • Option 1: Scrambled eggs with spinach and tomatoes, whole-grain bread, fresh fruit (e.g., banana or apple), yogurt
  • Option 2: Poha (flattened rice) with vegetables and peanuts, a glass of buttermilk
Mid-Morning Snack Mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts), yogurt with honey
Lunch
  • Option 1: Roti (whole wheat flatbread) with grilled chicken or paneer (cottage cheese), brown rice, mixed vegetable curry, and salad
  • Option 2: Rice with dal (lentils), mixed vegetable sabzi (curry), and curd
Afternoon Snack Whole-grain crackers with hummus, sliced cucumber, and carrots
Pre-Training Snack Banana or apple, whole-grain bread with peanut butter
During Training/Match Stay hydrated with water or nimbu pani (lemonade) with a pinch of salt and sugar
Post-Training/Match
  • Option 1: Protein shake (whey or plant-based), whole-grain sandwich with chicken or tofu and veggies
  • Option 2: Lassi (yogurt-based drink) with a protein-rich snack like sprouts or roasted chana (chickpeas)
Dinner
  • Option 1: Grilled fish or tofu, brown rice or roti, mixed greens salad
  • Option 2: Khichdi (rice and lentil porridge) with a side of curd and boiled vegetables
Evening Snack Curd (yogurt) with fresh seasonal fruits or a small piece of cheese, herbal tea or warm milk (to aid sleep)

General Tips:

  • Focus on locally available and easily understandable foods.
  • Prioritize whole grains (roti, rice), lentils (dal), vegetables, and fruits.
  • Minimize sugary and processed foods.
  • Maintain balanced meals.
  • Pay attention to portion sizes.
  • Stay well-hydrated with water or homemade beverages.
  • Allow time for digestion before intense training or matches.
